## Discussion Synthesis

### What Went Well

- Deterministic generator architecture scaled cleanly through all five stories
- Team handled a difficult infrastructure-quality phase without derailing Epic completion
- Once legacy errors were treated as shared system debt, they stopped reappearing in later stories
- Selection generator implementation expanded true end-to-end usefulness (not just isolated generator functions)

### Challenges

- Early Epic 5 progress included friction from old TypeScript/test issues that were initially treated as out-of-scope noise
- Minor legacy defects can compound if repeatedly deferred during feature delivery

### Key Insights

1. **Root-cause stabilization beats local patching**: focused cleanup removed recurring interruptions
2. **Deferred legacy issues are hidden delivery risk**: they resurface at inconvenient points unless explicitly tracked
3. **Quality gates are strongest when explicit**: “mark and triage” discipline is needed, not implicit memory
