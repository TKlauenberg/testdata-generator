## Successes and Strengths

1. **Codebase quality stayed high while the model became more complex**
   - The epic increased conceptual scope without obvious architectural drift or quality collapse.

2. **The configuration model became explicit and coherent**
   - Global, workspace, schema, and field-level behavior now fit one understandable system.

3. **Quality held across packages and surfaces**
   - CLI config loading, core precedence semantics, BDD coverage, command behavior, and documentation all advanced together.

4. **Review discipline improved the final implementation materially**
   - Late issues were addressed rather than waived, which kept the final state strong.

5. **Epic 8 follow-through was largely successful**
   - The retrospective commitments around canonical configuration, context-setting clarity, and explainability were meaningfully addressed.

---

## Challenges and Growth Areas

1. **Too much important discovery still happened during review**
   - Several stories reached their final shape only after review surfaced missing edge cases, unclear behavior, or insufficient proof.

2. **Acceptance criteria were not always proved clearly through BDD**
   - The team had strong testing overall, but the Gherkin layer did not always map tightly enough to the intended user-visible contract.

3. **Behavioral proof lagged behind implementation in some stories**
   - The mechanism was often implemented correctly, but the clearest acceptance-facing validation came later than it should have.

4. **Sprint-tracker hygiene still lagged execution reality**
   - Story completion was accurate, but epic-level sprint status did not reflect actual completion until retrospective closure.

---

## Key Insights and Lessons Learned

1. **Strong final quality does not excuse late discovery**
   - Review should harden work, not serve as the main place where important behavioral or edge-case gaps are first detected.

2. **BDD coverage needs to prove the acceptance criteria, not just exercise the feature**
   - The biggest process improvement opportunity is a tighter AC-to-Gherkin mapping before implementation begins.

3. **Configuration work needs explainability and proof at the same time**
   - For layered behavior, implementation, diagnostics, and test evidence have to stay aligned.

4. **Tracker accuracy matters at epic closure**
   - Process visibility should match implementation reality, especially when retrospectives depend on tracker state for discovery.
