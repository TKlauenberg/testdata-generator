## Challenges and Growth Areas

1. **Cross-cutting feature complexity**
   - Template logic, semantic validation, ordering, and generation execution are tightly coupled and require careful sequencing.

2. **Late discovery risk in semantic edge cases**
   - Some analyzer gaps became visible during implementation/review, indicating need for earlier edge-case capture in story prep.

3. **Error-message consistency across layers**
   - Multiple fixes focused on clarity and consistency of error communication between analyzer and generator/runtime surfaces.

---

## Key Insights and Lessons Learned

1. **Dependency-aware generation must be first-class, not incidental**
   - Field ordering, template references, and relationship expansion work best when treated as one explicit system.

2. **Defensive runtime guards still matter even with strong analysis**
   - Analyzer guarantees are strong, but runtime integrity checks improved diagnosability and resilience.

3. **Review quality compounds when findings are explicitly tracked and closed**
   - Tagging and resolving review items in-story improved final implementation confidence.

4. **BDD scenarios are most valuable when they assert complete record behavior, not partial outcomes**
   - This reduced false confidence and strengthened behavior-level guarantees.

### Dependencies on Epic 6

Epic 7 depends on Epic 6 outcomes in these areas:

- Template-derived values must cooperate with uniqueness checks.
- Dependency-ordered generation is required for uniqueness checks on derived/composite data.
- Relationship-generated nested records increase uniqueness surface area and collision complexity.

### Preparation Needs

- Define uniqueness behavior for template-derived fields and relationship-nested values.
- Align analyzer diagnostics and runtime uniqueness failure messages.

- Establish memory/footprint observation strategy for tracker growth on large generation sessions.

### Technical Prerequisites

- `UniquenessTracker` design for single-field and composite keys.
